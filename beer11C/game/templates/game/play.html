💡 Decision hint
{% if role == 'retailer' %} Retailer — Ordering from Wholesaler You are closest to real demand. Your upstream order takes 2 weeks to arrive, so you need to think 2 weeks ahead. Ask yourself:
  • What is my current demand trend?
  • How much is already in transit (pipeline)?
  • Do I have a backlog that needs covering?
A good starting heuristic: order = demand + backlog − goods already in transit. {% elif role == 'wholesaler' %} Wholesaler — Ordering from Distributor You see the Retailer's orders, not real customer demand. Those orders may already be inflated. Be careful not to over-react to a single large order.
  • Is the spike from the Retailer a real demand change or panic-ordering?
  • Check how much is already in your pipeline before adding more.
  • Smoothing your orders reduces bullwhip amplification upstream.
Tip: if you have goods in transit that cover next week's expected demand, you can order less. {% elif role == 'distributor' %} Distributor — Ordering from Factory You are two links from real demand. Variability reaching you is already amplified. Your goal is to buffer and absorb, not to amplify further.
  • Resist large swings — smooth your orders toward recent averages.
  • Count in-transit units before deciding how much to order.
  • Factory has a 3-week lag — plan ahead, but don't panic-order.
{% elif role == 'factory' %} Factory — Starting Production Your production pipeline is the longest in the chain (3 weeks total). Overproducing is costly; underproducing causes chain-wide backlog.
  • Check how much is already in the 2-week production pipeline.
  • Only start what you actually need, accounting for in-progress production.
  • The AI policy uses: order = max(0, 16 − inventory − in_transit + backlog).
Base-stock formula: target − inventory − in-transit + backlog. {% endif %}
