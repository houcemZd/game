🎓 Scenario Packs

One-click teaching scenarios — fills in the demand schedule and initial state automatically. Each scenario is designed to highlight a specific supply-chain concept.

📖 Classic MIT Step
Demand: 4 units/week for weeks 1–4, then 8 forever. The original Beer Game setup — demonstrates bullwhip from a single demand step.
📈 Demand Spike
Demand: 4,4,4,4,20,8,8,8,8,8 then 8 repeating. An extreme one-off surge — explores panic ordering and over-correction.
🌊 Seasonal Demand
Demand: 4,4,8,8,4,4,8,8 repeating (every 4 weeks). Shows how predictable seasonality is still amplified by the bullwhip.
🛑 Supply Disruption
Demand: 4,4,4,4,0,0,0,8,8,8 then 8 repeating. A sudden demand collapse followed by a rebound — stresses backlog and over-ordering.
📉 Gradual Growth
Demand: 2,3,4,5,6,7,8,9,10 then 10 repeating. Slow linear growth — tests whether pipeline awareness prevents runaway backlog.
🎲 Noisy Demand
Demand: 4,6,3,7,5,8,4,6,5,7 then repeating. Random-looking variation around a stable mean — highlights how noise gets amplified.
